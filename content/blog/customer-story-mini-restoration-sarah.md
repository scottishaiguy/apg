Some projects are more than just mechanical work – they're about preserving memories and family heritage. This is Sarah's story of bringing her grandfather's beloved Mini back to life.

## The Beginning: A Forgotten Treasure

"I found it tucked away in my grandad's garage after he passed away," Sarah recalls, her voice thick with emotion. "A 1972 Mini Clubman, covered in dust but still beautiful. He always said this car was his pride and joy."

Sarah, a primary school teacher from Leith, inherited the Mini along with her grandfather's Edinburgh home. The car hadn't been driven in over fifteen years, sitting silently as a monument to family memories.

### The Challenge
- **Vehicle:** 1972 Mini Clubman
- **Last driven:** 2009
- **Condition:** Dust-covered, seized engine, flat tyres
- **Goal:** Full restoration to driving condition

## Initial Assessment at APG

When Sarah first brought the Mini to our workshop, our team knew this wasn't just another restoration job.

"The way she spoke about her grandad, about the family holidays they took in this little car – it was clear this meant everything to her," recalls John, our senior mechanic.

### Our Initial Findings
- **Engine:** Seized due to long-term storage
- **Brakes:** Corroded and completely seized
- **Electrical:** Wiring brittle, many components failed
- **Body:** Surprisingly solid with minimal rust
- **Interior:** Original and well-preserved

## The Restoration Journey

### Phase 1: Mechanical Resurrection

**Engine Work**
The 1275cc A-series engine was carefully dismantled in our workshop. Each component was inspected, cleaned, and assessed.

"We found surprisingly little wear," explains our engine specialist. "The main issue was years of sitting without proper preservation."

**What we replaced:**
- Piston rings and bearings
- Timing chain and tensioner
- Water pump and thermostat
- All gaskets and seals
- Fuel lines and carburettor rebuild

**Brake System**
The entire braking system needed replacement:
- New brake lines (custom-bent for Mini)
- Rebuilt brake calipers
- New brake discs and pads
- Master cylinder replacement

### Phase 2: Electrical Modernisation

While preserving the classic character, we updated critical safety systems:

**Modern additions:**
- Electronic ignition for reliability
- Upgraded alternator
- LED bulbs for better visibility
- Modern fuses for safety
- Hidden Bluetooth stereo (Sarah's request!)

**What we preserved:**
- Original dashboard layout
- Classic switchgear
- Period-correct instruments
- Original steering wheel

### Phase 3: Body and Interior

**Bodywork**
The Mini's body was remarkably solid, typical of Scottish garage-kept cars:
- Minor dent removal
- Full respray in original British Racing Green
- Chrome bumpers refurbished
- New rubber seals and weatherstripping

**Interior Restoration**
"We kept everything original," Sarah insisted. "Even the slight wear on the driver's seat – that's where my grandad sat."

Our work focused on preservation:
- Deep cleaning and conditioning
- New carpet sets (original specification)
- Headliner replacement
- Door card refurbishment

## The Emotional Moment: First Start

After three months of meticulous work, the day came to start the engine for the first time.

"I was so nervous," Sarah admits. "John had me turn the key while he watched the gauges. When that little engine fired up, I just burst into tears. It sounded exactly like I remembered."

The entire workshop team gathered around. For us, it's moments like these that make our job meaningful.

## The Test Drive: Memory Lane

Sarah's first drive was planned carefully – a route through Edinburgh that mirrored family journeys from her childhood.

"We drove past Holyrood Park, where Grandad used to take us for ice cream in the summer. The little car handled perfectly, just like he always said it would."

**Technical achievements:**
- Smooth idle at proper temperature
- Responsive acceleration
- Straight tracking and braking
- All electrical systems functioning

## The Reveal: Family Reaction

Sarah organised a small family gathering to reveal the restored Mini.

"My mum just cried when she saw it," Sarah remembers. "She said it looked exactly like it did when she was young. Even my uncle, who's a mechanic himself, was impressed with the work."

### Technical Specifications (Final)
- **Engine:** 1275cc A-series, fully rebuilt
- **Power:** ~60 bhp (original specification)
- **Transmission:** 4-speed manual, rebuilt
- **Brakes:** Front disc, rear drum (upgraded)
- **Electrical:** 12V with modern safety features
- **Paint:** British Racing Green (original colour)

## Sarah's Advice for Others

For anyone considering a family restoration project, Sarah offers this advice:

**1. Document Everything**
"Take photos of everything before you start. You'll want to remember how it looked."

**2. Choose Your Workshop Wisely**
"Find a team that understands the emotional side, not just the mechanics. APG treated my Mini like it was their own family heirloom."

**3. Be Patient**
"Good restoration takes time. Rush leads to mistakes and regret."

**4. Stay Involved**
"I visited every week. Seeing the progress helped me feel connected to the process."
